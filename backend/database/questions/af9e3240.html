Electra is a circa 420–410 BCE play by Sophocles, translated in 1870 by R.C. Jebb. Electra, who is in mourning for her dead father and her long-absent brother, is aware of the intensity of her grief but believes it to be justified: ______blank

Which quotation from Electra most effectively illustrates the claim?

  1. “O thou pure sunlight, and thou air, earth’s canopy, how often have ye heard the strains of my lament, the wild blows dealt against this bleeding breast, when dark night fails!”

  2. “Send to me my brother; for I have no more the strength to bear up alone against the load of grief that weighs me down.”

  3. “I know my own passion, it escapes me not; but, seeing that the causes are so dire, will never curb these frenzied plaints, while life is in me.” 

  4. “But never will I cease from dirge and sore lament, while I look on the trembling rays of the bright stars, or on this light of day.”

Tip: Press CTRL/Command to toggle answer
Correct Answer: C
Rationale

Choice C is the best answer. Electra states that she “knows her own passion,” which shows that she’s aware of the intensity of her grief. But she also claims that the “causes are so dire”—meaning the reasons for her grief are so awful—that she can’t let it go, which shows that she believes her grief is justified.
